Research Profile

Research Directions

As a computational biologist, my research is dedicated to advancing the field of cancer treatment through the integration of multiomics, computational structural biology, and state-of-the-art machine and deep learning techniques. My work focuses on utilizing multiomics data—encompassing genomics, proteomics, and transcriptomics—to develop comprehensive models that elucidate the molecular mechanisms underlying cancer. A significant aspect of my research is centered around drug discovery and repurposing, where I employ advanced computational methods to identify novel therapeutic agents and evaluate existing drugs for new uses. Additionally, I am deeply engaged in predicting and classifying drug pair cell synergy, leveraging deep learning algorithms to uncover effective drug combinations that enhance treatment efficacy. This holistic approach not only provides valuable insights into cancer biology but also paves the way for innovative therapeutic strategies, ultimately aiming to transform cancer treatment and improve patient outcomes through precision medicine.
